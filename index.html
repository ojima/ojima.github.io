Fiction

Here is a list of all short fiction unconnected to any great narratives I have written in chronological order. A lot of these are unfinished, as I am terrible at continuing to write on something once I put my mind at something else. Apologies.

  • 2020, The Necrogon (Unfinished)
    My first attempt at something other than (high) fantasy: a more Lovecraftian-like horror story.

  • 2020, Ordained (Unfinished)
    Attempt to write a psychological thriller: three astronauts get stranded in space and with no way to get saved, they lose contact with the Earth.

  • 2020, The Door (Unfinished)
    Another short idea I had for a Lovecraftian horror story about a world where apparitions and ghostly anomalies are real - a woman is being followed by a supernatural experience and seeks a way to release herself from it.

Hattusa (Bronze Age Fantasy)

Here is a list of all fiction I have written within "Hattusa" a more recent fantasy world I began in 2020 inspired by the Late Bronze Age in the Near Middle-East.

  • 2020―2021, Blades of Copper and Bronze
    More of a high fantasy story set in the late bronze age. The idea is getting a bit long while the main body is taking a while to catch up, so once I get more written down I probably will copy it to here.

  • 2020, Kadesh
    "Kadesh" tells a fictional account of the Battle of Kadesh (1274 BC), where the Kemetian army, while on campaign in the Hattic lands near the border town of Kadesh, managed to avoid being destroyed in a surprise attack.

  • 2020, Akhnaten
    The Kemetian Pharaoh Amenhotep has a prophetic dream in which the Sun god Aten tells him of a bright future for his followers. Amenhotep dons the name Akhnaten, "Effective for Aten", and fights his way through the conservative Kemetian priesthoods who refuse to abandon their old faiths.

Linguistics of the Ancient Near East

I greatly enjoy linguistics, notably the languages of the Ancient Near East. In my endeavours to learn more about this fascinating subject, I occasionally translate some texts. I list some of these below here.

Old Persian

Translations of a variety of Old Persian texts by me. Yes, I have also translated the Behistun Inscription (DB), as well as a number of others, and if I get to it, I will put those translations on here as well.

  • 2018, Translation of the Arsames Hamadan (AsH) Inscription
    A golden tablet, excavated in Hamadan, Iran, in 1920. The tablet contains 14 lines of Old Persian cuneiform inscription, and is attributed to Arsames, son of Ariaramnes, and grandfather to the later king Darius I of the Achaemenid empire.

  • 2018, Translation of the Xerxes Van (XV) Inscription
    A wall inscription attributed to Xerxes I (c. 518 ― 465 BC), king of kings of the Achaemenid empire. The inscription is found at the fortress near Van, modern-day Turkey.

  • 2020, Translation of the Artaxerxes III Inscription at Persepolis (A3Pa)
    A wall inscription attributed to Artaxerxes III (??? - 338 BC) next to a stone staircase. This text in particular was transcribed and translated by me directly from a high-resolution color photo, instead of from a transcription from another author.

In early 2021, I started studying Ugaritic using Stanislav Segert's A Basic Grammar of the Ugaritic Language. I am tracking my progress with a variety of translations, some of which are shared here.
